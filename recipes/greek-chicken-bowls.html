Greek Chicken Bowls

These simple greek chicken bowls pack a lot of flavor and textures into one easy to make meal. Thes bowls are perfect for meal prep, loaded with veggies and homemade tzatziki.

Recipe Credit: Claire Cary

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 4 small chicken breasts, about 1 1/4 pounds
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp lemon zest
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 Tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes

Bowl

  • 2 cups cooked rice or quinoa
  • 2 cups halved grape or cherr tomatoes
  • 2 cups diced or chopped cucumber
  • 4 cups shredded romaine lettuce
  • 1 cup sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup feta

Tzatziki

  • 1 cup plain greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup grated cucumber
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1/4 tsp salt, or to taste
  • 1 Tbsp fresh chopped dill

Directions

  1. Whisk together all ingredients for the chicken marinade.
  2. Pound the chicken to about 1/2 inch thick, and add to a shallow bowl or ziplock bag with the marinade.
  3. Let marinade for at least 30 minutes, or up to a few hours.
  4. Meanwhile, combine all ingredients for tzatziki and set aside.
  5. Prep the rice and veggies. Feel free to add pitted olives to each bowl as well.
  6. Once the chicken is done marinating, preheat your air fryer to 380 and air fry on one side for 7 minutes, then flip over and cook for 3-4 more minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 165 F.
  7. If you do not have an air fryer, heat a skillet with oil or butter over medium/low heat and fry on one side for 7-8 minutes, then flip and fry on the other side until golden and the internal temperature reaches 165 F.
  8. Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes away from the heat, then slice.
  9. Assemble your bowls with the rice, veggies, sliced chicken, feta, and tzatziki. You can also drizzle on some olive oil and lemon juice as well. Enjoy!
