Tonkatsu (Japanese Pork Cutlet)

Recipe Credit: With A Blast

Savor the crunch with our authentic Tonkatsu recipe! Make this classic Japanese breaded pork cutlet, served with a tangy sauce.

Ingredients

Tonkatsu Sauce

  • 1/2 cup worcestershire sauce
  • 1/3 cup ketchup (thick)
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • Lemon juice (to taste)

Pork Schnitzels

  • 6 150 grams pork tenderloin cutlets (see not below)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 2 extra-large eggs (lightly beaten)
  • Flour (for coating)
  • 2 cups panko bread crumbs (or regular bread crumbs)
  • Vegetable oil (for shallow frying)

Directions

Tonkatsu Sauce

  1. Heat all the sauce ingredients for a bout 1 minute, strring constantly. Set aside.

Pork Schnitzels

  1. Seas pork schnitzels with salt and black pepper, then coat well in flour.
  2. Dip in egg and coat with bread crumbs. Refrigerate for 15 minutes to set.
  3. Heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet and fry the schnitzels about 3 minutes per side or until cooked through and golden brown.
  4. Remove from skillet and drain on paper towels. Serve drizzled with the sauce.

Notes

  • To make schnitzels from tenderloins, simply slice a 900 grams/2 pounds loin into 6 pieces - place the pieces of pork between baking paper and pund with a meat tenderizer until the desired size and thickness.
  • Substitute the pork with chicken to make Torikatsu!
